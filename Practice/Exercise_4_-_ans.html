Question 4

  1. Assume that the system crashes at B, what should be done to recover the system?

As the transaction graph indicated, the End Point of T1 and T2 is finished before the crush at B, which means that all the changes of T1 and T2 have been committed before B.

Therefore, T1, T2: redo T3: undo

  1. Assume a checkpoint is made at point A, what should be done to the three transactions when the crash happens at B?

If the checkpoint has been made at point A, it means all the operations and data before A has been backup. Thus, all the operation of T1 has been safely recorded. If crushing happens at B, the system will recover to the checkpoint A. At the same time, the End Point of T2 is finished before the crush at B, T2 can be safely redo.

Therefore,

T2: redo

T3: undo
